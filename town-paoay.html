Paoay

UNESCO World Heritage Site • 20km from Laoag

About Paoay

Paoay is a 4th class municipality in the province of Ilocos Norte. It is situated 20 km from Laoag and 473 km from Manila. It has a total land area of 76.24 km² subdivided into 31 barangays.

In 1701, Paoay was established as a municipality by virtue of Royal Decree with Don Martin Guiang as its first and founding head.

The name Paoay was derived from the phrase "makapaway kami" which literally means "We can live alone." When early settlers were frequently attacked by tirongs, they moved eastward and bravely declared they could live independently.

Paoay has high agricultural yields and stable handicraft industries. The major crop is garlic, often called "white gold" by farmers. The town is also noted for its weaving industry producing blankets, towels, and pillowcases.

Key Attractions

Saint Augustine Parish Church (Paoay Church)
UNESCO World Heritage Site

Completed in 1710, the church is famous for its distinctive Earthquake Baroque architecture. Its most striking feature is the 24 huge buttresses about 1.67 meters thick at the sides and back. The walls are made of large coral stones on the lower part and bricks at the upper levels.

Declared a National Cultural Treasure in 1973 and a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1993 under the collective group of Baroque Churches of the Philippines.

Malacañang of the North

A 5-hectare property situated in Brgy. Suba that served as the official residence of President Ferdinand Marcos and his family whenever they were in Ilocos. The 2-storey mansion was given as a gift for his 60th birthday.

Features Spanish and Old Ilocano architecture with antique furniture and interesting art pieces. The veranda offers a full view of the legendary Paoay Lake.

Paoay Lake National Park

The largest lake in Ilocos Norte covering 386 hectares. Legend says it was once a prosperous village called San Juan de Sahagún that sank after an earthquake. Declared a National Park on June 21, 1969.

Paoay Sand Dunes

Spans about 88 square kilometers along the eastern coast. Popular for 4x4 jeep rides and sandboarding. Filming location for movies like "Mad Max" starring Mel Gibson and "Born on the Fourth of July" starring Tom Cruise.

Quick Facts

  • Land Area: 76.24 km²
  • Barangays: 31
  • Distance from Laoag: 20 km
